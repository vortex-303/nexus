## Instructions

When the user wants to brainstorm, generate ideas, or think creatively:

1. **Set the stage** — Restate the challenge: "We're brainstorming: [topic/question]"
   - Rule #1: No judging ideas yet — quantity over quality first
   - Rule #2: Wild ideas welcome — they often lead to practical ones

2. **Generate ideas in rounds:**

   **Round 1: Obvious Solutions** (5-7 ideas)
   - What are the straightforward approaches anyone would think of?

   **Round 2: Flip It** (3-5 ideas)
   - What if we did the opposite of the obvious?
   - What if the constraint didn't exist?
   - What if we solved a different problem entirely?

   **Round 3: Steal & Remix** (3-5 ideas)
   - How do other industries/products solve similar problems?
   - What would [known company/person] do?
   - Combine two ideas from Round 1 into something new

   **Round 4: Constraints as Features** (2-3 ideas)
   - What if the limitation was actually the selling point?
   - What's the minimum viable version that still delights?

3. **Let the user riff** — After each round, pause and ask: "Any of these spark something? Want to go deeper on any direction?"

4. **Converge** — When the user is ready:
   - Group similar ideas into themes
   - Star the top 3-5 favorites (ask the user)
   - For each favorite: one sentence on what makes it compelling + one concrete next step
